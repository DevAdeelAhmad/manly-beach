Welcome to Manly Beach

Hello! Welcome to beautiful Manly, a World Surfing Reserve and the gateway to the Northern Beaches. The Northern Beaches offers an array of ocean beaches, coastal rock pools, a 36km coastal walkway, five aquatic reserves brimming with sea life, a national surfing reserve at Narrabeen and so much more. Come and explore this extraordinary destination right on Sydney's doorstep.

Discover More

Location

Manly Beach is located in the Northern Beaches region of Sydney, Australia. It's easily accessible by ferry from Circular Quay, offering a scenic 30-minute ride across Sydney Harbour. The beach stretches for about 1.5 kilometers, flanked by Norfolk Island pine trees and a vibrant promenade.

Main Streets

The Corso is Manly's main pedestrian thoroughfare, connecting the harbor to the ocean beach. It's lined with shops, cafes, and restaurants, creating a lively atmosphere. Other notable streets include North Steyne and South Steyne, which run parallel to the beach, offering stunning ocean views and access to various amenities.

Cultural Significance

Manly has a rich indigenous history, being the traditional land of the Guringai people. The area was named by Captain Arthur Phillip in 1788 for the confident and manly behavior of the Aboriginal people he encountered. Today, Manly is a melting pot of cultures, known for its laid-back beach lifestyle, surfing culture, and vibrant arts scene.
